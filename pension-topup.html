Opening a SIPP

Popular providers include Vanguard, Fidelity, and Hargreaves Lansdown. You can open one in around 15 minutes online. All operate on a relief-at-sourceYou pay net (the amount after tax), they claim 20% from HMRC automatically. basis — you pay net, they claim the 20% automatically.

Just 3 more steps to go
Your tax relief

What you pay and what the government gives back.

How does relief at source work?
  • You transfer your net contribution from your bank account to the pension
  • Your pension provider claims 20% basic rate relief from HMRC and adds it automatically — usually within 6–10 weeks
  • If you're a higher or additional rate taxpayer, you claim the extra relief via Self Assessment
  • The full gross amount is then invested in your chosen fund

Higher rate taxpayer — claim the extra relief
You pay tax at 40% or 45%, so the government contributes more to your pension through tax relief.

Step 3 of 4 — almost done
Check your limits

Make sure your total contributions stay within HMRC's annual allowance.

What counts towards the annual allowance?
The total of your and your employer's contributions in a tax year. The government allows £60,000 (or 100% of earnings if less).

Both your own contributions and your employer's contributions count. For defined contribution schemes, it's simply the total paid in. The limit is £60,000 or 100% of your earnings, whichever is lower.

What is carry forward?
Unused allowance from the past 3 years that you can use this year to contribute more than £60,000.

If you've been a pension scheme member for the past three years and haven't used your full allowance, you can carry forward unused allowance — potentially allowing contributions well above £60,000 this year. Speak to a financial adviser to use this correctly.

What to do now.

    Claim the extra relief via Self Assessment

    If you're a higher rate taxpayer, you get extra relief. Register and file a tax return to claim it.

    • 1
      Register for Self Assessment
      Go to gov.uk and select "register for Self Assessment".
    • 2
      Get your UTR
      HMRC posts it to you — wait 10 working days.
    • 3
      File your tax return
      Enter your gross contribution and HMRC calculates your refund.

    Deadlines

    Register by 5 October. File by 31 January.
